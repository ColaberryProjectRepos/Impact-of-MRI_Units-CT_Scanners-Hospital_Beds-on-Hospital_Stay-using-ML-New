Optimize MRI Units and CT Scanners Allocation

Objective

To reduce the average duration of hospital stays by optimizing the allocation of MRI Units and CT Scanners, leading to improved patient throughput and increased hospital capacity.

Analysis
Positive Insight: An increase in MRI Units by over 15% year-over-year is associated with a reduction in the actual hospital stay duration by up to 0.5 days in some locations. This suggests that better availability of MRI services can lead to faster diagnosis and treatment plans.



Negative Insight: Locations with a slower increase in CT Scanners (below 5% YoY) have shown a less significant decrease in hospital stay durations, indicating that inadequate growth in CT scanning capacity may hinder potential improvements in patient management efficiency.


ROI Calculation


Assuming an average daily cost for hospital stay is $1,500, reducing the average stay by 0.5 days across 1000 patients annually can save up to $750,000. Investing in additional MRI units, with an average cost of $2 million per unit, can break even in approximately 2.7 years, not accounting for additional revenue generated from increased patient capacity.
